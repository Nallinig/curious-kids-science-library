Meet the White Rhino! 🦏
The White Rhino is one of the largest land animals on Earth — even bigger than a car! 🚗

Despite the name, they are not really white — they are grey! The name probably came from the Dutch word "wijd" meaning "wide" — because of their wide, flat mouth! 😄

They live on the grassy plains of Africa and love to graze on grass all day long. 🌾

Life on the African Plains 🌍
White Rhinos spend their days peacefully grazing on grass in the savannahs of Africa — countries like South Africa, Kenya, Zimbabwe and Uganda. 🌾

They love to wallow in mud to cool down and protect their skin from the hot sun! 🌞

Mother rhinos are incredibly devoted — a baby calf stays with its mum for 2 to 3 years!

Rhinos are actually gentle animals — they only charge when they feel threatened! 🦏💛
😢
The Problem: Their Magnificent Horn 😢
The rhino's horn is made of keratin — the same stuff as your fingernails! It is NOT magic, it is NOT medicine, it is just keratin.

But sadly, some people believed the horn had special powers and paid huge amounts of money for it. This led to terrible poaching — illegal hunting of rhinos just for their horns. 💔

For many years, thousands of rhinos were killed — and their numbers fell dramatically.

Today, strict laws and brave rangers protect them every single day. 💪

Science is Trying to Save Them! 🔬
Scientists have done something extraordinary — they froze the cells of Northern White Rhinos before they died! 🧊

Using these frozen cells, they are trying to create new embryos — baby rhinos — in a laboratory! 🍼

They hope a Southern White Rhino surrogate mother could carry and give birth to a Northern White Rhino baby! 🦏💛

It has never been done before — but scientists believe it is possible! 🌟
🛡️
The Heroes Protecting Rhinos 🛡️
Around the world, thousands of brave rangers patrol game reserves day and night to protect rhinos from poachers. 🌙☀️

In South Africa, some rhinos have their horns painted or injected with a safe substance that makes them useless — so poachers leave them alone! 💉

Drones and cameras now watch over rhino habitats from the sky! 🚁

And the Southern White Rhino — once down to just 50 animals — is now back to 20,000! That is what protection can do! 🎉
💜
Najin and Fatu 💜
The last two Northern White Rhinos on Earth are a mother and daughter — Najin and Fatu — living safely at Ol Pejeta Conservancy in Kenya. 🇰🇪

They are guarded by armed rangers 24 hours a day — some of the most protected animals on the planet! 🛡️

Najin and Fatu are gentle and curious — they know their keepers by name and love being brushed! 🪮💛

Their story has touched hearts around the world — including yours! 🌍💜
