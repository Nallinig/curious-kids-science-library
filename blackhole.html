First — What is a Star? ☀️
You know our Sun? That big bright ball that lights up our day? Our Sun is a star!
Stars are giant balls of super-hot gas that glow and shine for billions of years.
There are more stars in space than grains of sand on ALL of Earth's beaches! 🏖️
Stars are born, they live for a very long time, and then... they die. And when a really BIG star dies, something incredible happens! 🌟
How is a Black Hole Born? 💥
Imagine a really, really HUGE star — much bigger than our Sun.
When this giant star gets old and runs out of fuel, it goes BOOM! — a giant explosion called a supernova! 💥
After the explosion, all the leftover stuff squishes together into a tiny, tiny point.
This squishing is SO powerful that it creates a black hole — the most powerful thing in the universe! ⚫

What IS a Black Hole? ⚫
A black hole is like a giant invisible vacuum cleaner in space!
It pulls everything nearby towards it — gas, dust, even light!
That's why it looks completely black — no light can escape from it, so we can't see inside! 🔦
Think of it like a drain in a bathtub — everything swirls around and gets pulled in! 🛁
Fun fact: it's not actually a hole! It's a tiny ball of super-squished stuff — we just call it a "hole" because nothing can get out! 😄
📏
How Big Are They? 📏
Black holes come in different sizes — just like dogs! 🐕
Small ones can be as tiny as a city — but weigh more than our whole Sun!
Giant ones called supermassive black holes are at the centre of almost every galaxy — including our own Milky Way! 🌌
The one in our galaxy is called Sagittarius A* and it's 4 million times heavier than our Sun! 🤯

Does it Eat Everything? 😋
Only things that get very close get pulled in!
Our Earth is safe — we are very, very far from any black hole. 🌍✅
The closest black hole to us is 1,500 light years away — that means even travelling at the speed of light, it would take 1,500 years to get there!
So no need to worry — you are perfectly safe! 😊
Black holes mostly eat gas and dust floating nearby in space. 🌫️
🔭
Can We See a Black Hole? 🔭
Since no light escapes, we can't see the black hole itself — but we can see its shadow!
In 2019, scientists took the very first photo of a black hole! 📸
It looked like a glowing orange ring with a dark circle in the middle — like a cosmic donut! 🍩
It took over 200 scientists from all around the world working together to get that one amazing photo! 🌍🤝

Wild Space Facts! 🤩
Ready for some mind-blowing facts?
⏰ Near a black hole, time goes slower! If you could survive next to one, you'd age more slowly than people on Earth!
🍝 Scientists call getting stretched by a black hole "spaghettification" — you'd get stretched like a noodle!
🌌 There are billions of black holes in our galaxy alone — but they're so far away they're harmless!
