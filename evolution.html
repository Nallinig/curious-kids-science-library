It All Began in the Ocean! 🌊
A very, VERY long time ago — about 4 billion years ago — Earth was covered mostly in water.
Tiny, tiny living things called microbes appeared in the ocean. They were so small you'd need a microscope to see them! 🦠
These little guys were the very first life on Earth — the great-great-great (times a million!) grandparents of every living thing today. 🎉

What is Evolution? 🌱
Imagine your family. You look a little like your mom or dad, right? But not exactly the same!
Evolution is how living things slowly change over millions and millions of years. Each baby is just a tiny bit different from its parents.
The ones who are best at finding food, staying safe, and having babies of their own survive. Little by little, after millions of babies, animals can look very different! 🐣

Fish Crawled onto Land! 🦎
After millions of years, some fish started growing little leg-like fins. They could wiggle up onto land!
These turned into reptiles and amphibians — things like frogs, lizards, and dinosaurs!
🦕 Dinosaurs ruled the Earth for 165 million years — way longer than humans have been around. Then, about 66 million years ago, a giant asteroid hit Earth... and most dinosaurs disappeared. But some survived and became... birds! 🐦

Mammals Appear! 🐾
After the dinosaurs, small furry mammals got their chance to grow and spread everywhere!
Mammals are animals that are warm and fuzzy, drink their mama's milk as babies, and are warm-blooded — like dogs, elephants, whales, and YOU! 🐋
Over millions of years, mammals evolved into all sorts of creatures. Some went back to the sea and became whales. Some grew wings. And some... climbed into the trees. 🐒

Our Ancient Relatives 🐒
Here's something amazing: humans and chimpanzees share 98% of the same DNA! That's like being almost-identical recipes with just a few ingredients changed.
We didn't come from chimps though — it's more like we have the same great-great-grandparents! About 6 million years ago, our family tree branched apart.
Slowly, our ancestors stood upright, grew bigger brains, learned to use tools, make fire, and tell stories. 🔥

How Do Scientists Know? 🔬
Great question! Scientists are like nature's detectives, and they have amazing clues:
🦴 Fossils — bones and shells turned to stone, millions of years old!
🧬 DNA — the tiny instructions inside every cell that show who's related to who!
🌍 Rock layers — Earth keeps records like a history book in stone!
Thousands of scientists all over the world have checked these clues for over 150 years. Evolution is one of the best-proven ideas in all of science! ✅

Keep asking questions. Keep being curious. That's what scientists do — and maybe YOU will be one too! 🚀🔭
